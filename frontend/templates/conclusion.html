Conclusion & Factor Explanation
‍

Our project harnesses the power of machine learning to deliver a fast, intelligent, and accessible tool for identifying diabetes and determining its type — Type 1 or Type 2. Designed with real-world healthcare challenges in mind, this model supports early diagnosis, even in settings where lab access may be limited or delayed.
Key Factors Driving the Model

Age

A major determinant in diabetes type: Type 1 is more common in younger individuals, while Type 2 risk increases with age.

Fasting Blood Glucose

Provides an immediate snapshot of glucose control, reinforcing the accuracy of diabetes prediction.

HbA1c Level

A central biomarker reflecting average blood glucose over the past 2–3 months. Crucial for confirming and predicting diabetes status.

BMI (Obesity Indicator)

Higher BMI is closely associated with insulin resistance and is a significant predictor of Type 2 diabetes.

Hypertension & Heart Disease

Frequently co-existing with diabetes; these comorbidities strengthen the model's predictive confidence.

Age

A known contributor to metabolic and vascular damage, increasing the risk of developing Type 2 diabetes.

Gender

Used to identify potential patterns in diabetes risk, although not a direct causal factor.

What Makes This Model Different?
Unlike a single lab test, this model integrates clinical, biological, and lifestyle indicators to deliver a more holistic and accurate prediction of diabetes risk and type. It's designed to enhance medical decision-making, especially where rapid screening is essential.
