Predicting Risk Before Clinical Onset
‍

Individuals can have normal HbA1c today but still be on a high-risk trajectory toward diabetes.
‍
Our model analyzes a combination of early indicators:

  • Age
  • Fasting blood glucose
  • Hypertension
  • Smoking history
  • BMI (obesity)
  • Heart disease history


    These allow for proactive intervention before HbA1c even crosses diagnostic thresholds.

Diabetes Type Classification
(Type 1 vs. Type 2)
‍

HbA1c indicates elevated glucose but cannot specify the type of diabetes, our model intelligently infers diabetes type based on:

  • Age bracket
  • HbA1c level
  • Blood glucose profile

    This assists in guiding the right treatment strategy from the start.

Accessibility

Accessibility in Low-Resource or Remote Settings
In real-world conditions, HbA1c testing may not be feasible due to:

  • High cost
  • Lack of laboratory infrastructure
  • Long turnaround times
    This assists in guiding the right treatment strategy from the start.

    Our model provides a fast, low-cost, data-driven screening tool using easily available inputs like age, BMI, blood pressure, etc.

Scalable for Population-Level Screening
‍

Scalable for Population-Level Screening
Healthcare systems can:

  • Instantly analyze thousands of patient records
  • Flag high-risk individuals for follow-up HbA1c testing
  • Reduce unnecessary testing costs
  • Streamline resource allocation

    This makes the model valuable for preventive public health initiatives.

It’s Not a Replacement — It’s an Enhancement

Our model doesn’t aim to replace HbA1c testing. Instead, it acts as a clinical decision support tool that:

  • Enables early detection
  • Supports risk stratification
  • Suggests diabetes type
  • Optimizes care in low-access environments
